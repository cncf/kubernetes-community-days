# Running Organizer Meetings

These guidelines are here to help organizers run engaging, productive weekly or bi-weekly meetings. They are not enforced, just practical advice you can follow.

## Suggested Opening Statements

Consider starting every meeting by covering these items:
- **Reference the CNCF Code of Conduct** and emphasize respectful and inclusive behavior. The full CoC lives at [https://github.com/cncf/foundation/blob/master/code-of-conduct.md](https://github.com/cncf/foundation/blob/master/code-of-conduct.md)
- **Let attendees know the meeting is being recorded**, which promotes transparency and accountability.
- **Give new participants time to introduce themselves**, allowing existing attendees to welcome newcomers and foster community.
- **Introduce the moderator** for the meeting and note their name in the meeting agenda.

> MORE TIPS: [How To Moderate An Open Source Meeting](https://contribute.cncf.io/projects/best-practices/community/contributor-growth/meeting-moderation)

## Before the Meeting

- Share the meeting on both the **Slack channel and the mailing list** to maximize visibility
- **Set a public meeting agenda** in advance so participants can contribute discussion points, ensuring efficient and meaningful discussion
- **Choose a moderator** from regular attendees, ideally someone not heavily invested in specific ongoing discussions so they can remain impartial
- Consider **rotating the moderator role** among contributors to bring diverse perspectives

## During the Meeting

- Use **"Raise Your Hand" and speaker queuing** techniques so all voices get heard in an orderly way
- **Note attendees' names** in the meeting agenda, including those who announced absences
- **Monitor chat** and repeat/address questions for those who can't use audio/video
- Have the moderator keep an eye on **timekeeping** to help ensure all agenda items get covered
- **Capture action items** and the people responsible for them directly in the agenda doc

## Handling Issues

If a Code of Conduct issue arises during the meeting, the CNCF CoC Committee can be reached at [conduct@cncf.io](mailto:conduct@cncf.io). 

[Link to CoC](https://github.com/cncf/foundation/blob/master/code-of-conduct.md)

There are also some KCD-specific considerations worth keeping in mind for organizer meetings:

## Organizer Meeting Cadence

CNCF suggests starting with **bi-weekly planning meetings**, then ramping up to **weekly meetings** as the event date approaches. Once approved, CNCF staff will schedule **monthly syncs** with your team via a private Slack channel dedicated to KCD organizers globally. 

[About Organizing](about-organizing.md)

## Organizer Eligibility Requirements

Before holding official organizing meetings, your team should satisfy these terms: 
- Minimum of **3 organizers from 3 different organizations**
- At least **one organizer should be a CNCF Ambassador or CNCF project maintainer**
- The **majority of organizers should live in or near the KCD location** (up to 1/4 can be from outside the country)
- Every organizer should have an account on **Cloud Native Community Groups**
- All organizers are encouraged to complete the **[Inclusive Open Source Community Orientation (LFC102)](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/training/inclusive-open-source-community-orientation-lfc102/)** course
- All organizers should agree to the **CNCF Code of Conduct**
- There is **no single "leader"** for a KCD — the team shares responsibility

[Host a KCD](../README.md)

## What to Cover in Check-in Meetings

CNCF provides a **[Conference Planning Action Items and Checklist](https://github.com/cncf/kubernetes-community-days/tree/main/planning)** (Google Spreadsheet) as a helpful template for check-in meetings. Consider tracking progress on these areas:
- Finance and budgeting
- Marketing and promotion
- Sponsors
- CFP and schedule
- Website and design
- Registration
- Venue, catering, and logistics
- Swag

## Key Event Standards to Reinforce

It's worth reminding your team periodically that:
- KCDs are **primarily for community engagement**, and both organizers and speakers should avoid vendor pitches
- Any tools or platforms referenced in talks should be **open source**
- If the event turns a profit, **it should be reinvested in the community** (organizers can only be reimbursed for expenses, not their time)
- The **attendee list should not be used for commercial or marketing purposes**

[About Organizing](about-organizing.md)

## Content Management Meetings

CNCF recommends that **organizers avoid submitting talks to their own KCD**. Content review meetings might include:
- Regular speaker check-ins to track progress
- Offering presentation coaching and content development assistance
- Reviewing presentations in advance
- Scheduling **at least two live video run-throughs** before the event
- Identifying all A/V requirements per talk

[Content Management](../committee-resources/content-management.md)
